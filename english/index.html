Back Stretches for Expecting Mothers

Benefits

These gentle back stretches help relieve tension caused by your growing belly and changing center of gravity.

Safety Tips

  • Move slowly and with control
  • Avoid lying flat on your back after first trimester
  • Listen to your body - don't push too far
  • Use props like pillows for support
1

Seated Cat-Cow Stretch

Sit comfortably on a chair or cushion. Inhale as you arch your back, lifting your chest (Cow). Exhale as you round your spine, tucking your chin (Cat). Repeat 5-8 times.

2

Side-Lying Stretch

Lie on your side with knees bent. Reach your top arm overhead, stretching through your side body. Hold for 3 breaths, then switch sides.

3

Supported Child's Pose

Kneel and place a pillow under your belly and chest. Stretch your arms forward and rest your forehead on the ground. Breathe deeply for 5-8 breaths.

5-Minute Daily Routines

Morning Routine

  1. Neck rolls (30 sec)
  2. Seated cat-cow (1 min)
  3. Side stretches (1 min per side)
  4. Pelvic tilts (1 min)
  5. Deep breathing (30 sec)

Evening Routine

  1. Seated forward fold (1 min)
  2. Side-lying stretch (1 min per side)
  3. Supported child's pose (1 min)
  4. Legs up the wall (1 min)
  5. Guided relaxation (1 min)

Tips for Success

  • Choose a consistent time each day
  • Use pillows and props for comfort
  • Focus on your breath throughout
  • Modify as needed for your comfort
  • Stay hydrated before and after
